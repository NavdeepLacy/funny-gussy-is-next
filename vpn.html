The F.U.N.G.U.S. VPN Guide

The school wifi has a number of restrictions that are harmful to it's users (you can read more about this in our FAQ and About page) and therefore it is almost required to use a vpn. However, not all vpn's work and there is some risk associated with them, therefore we have created this guide to help those in schools with BYOD access, or those who have accessed the guest network.

What is a VPN?

A VPN, which stands for Virtual Private Network, is an application or service that routes your traffic through a separate server. This is useful if you want an internet operator (our school in this case) to not have the ability to see your data or restrict access to the network.

Risks of a VPN

Routing your traffic through a VPN means that the VPN provider can see your activity if they don't apply the necessary restrictions, typically you want to avoid vpn's that don't have good policies. This takes time however, and requires diligent research.

Advantages of using a VPN on the school network

Using a VPN on the school network can be good for circumventing restrictions, as well as keeping the county from learning your browsing history. A vpn can also minimize the risk of being tracked down by a network admin for using the guest network (but does not eliminate it).

Disadvantages of using a VPN on the guest network

However, VPN's do come with a disadvantage, including the fact that depending on the vpn, you will need to pay a decent chunk of money if you want good data and fast speeds. Plus there's definitely going to be a slowdown in your internet speeds, but depending on the situation it can be negligible at best.

F.U.N.G.U.S. Reccomendations

(All of these vpns should give you a reasonable expectation of privacy, however we can't verify that there isn't an NSA black box connected to their servers. You likely don't need to worry about this but we wanted to at least warn you first)

Windscribe VPN

Windscribe's vpn service has a good free tier, plus we know it works with the school network (when you select IKEv2 as the protocol, or the connection method).

ProtonVPN

ProtonVPN also works on the school network (when logging in at home or on cellular) and it has a decent free plan too, with unlimited allowance of free data.
