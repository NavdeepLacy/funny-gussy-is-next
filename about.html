About Us

"On behalf of the Future, I ask you of the Past to leave us alone. You are not welcome among us. You have no sovereignty where we gather." -John Perry Barlow.

F.U.N.G.U.S is an invitation-only project that concerns itself with the recent GCPS decision to remove BYOD. We have three main goals.

1. Spread Awareness

While this is the least impressive of our goals, it is actually the most important. Most people are not informed of the spyware and immoral technology practices that the county implements on it's students. They do not inform people adequately, so we are doing their job for them. Informing people of these issues helps others safeguard their data, and is the most important goal of F.U.N.G.U.S.

2. Bypass Restrictions

Annoyed that Duckduckgo is blocked for no apparent reason? Annoyed that you cannot access signal to text your family members, or discord to talk to your friends? We hear you. Our second goal is to bypass the school's wifi restrictions. While we understand the necessity of wifi restrictions, (stuff like porn or illegal sites should not be allowed in any case) the filters used by GCPS are unreasonable and spotty at best, dangerous at worst. In fact the recent removal of the BYOD network for most schools during the 2022-2023 school year and all schools 2023-onward has only made this issue more apparent. Cellular cannot be accessed in many parts of the building, so what if there's a shooting? or another private personal problem. Or a student is being attacked by another student and no help is nearby? The solution that GCPS has presented is to use a school phone, but not only does this not work in many situations, like the one we just described, it can give the school more private info on students and parents, and will only increase the risk of your data being taken by the school, and breached in a security exploit (by the way, the school really needs to fix some of these, and needs to contact us using renahersch@gmail.com).

3. Protect our Data

Our last goal is to protect our data. Public schools have security breaches often, because they store such sensitive info but don't have the proper security protocols to protect it. Considering how many important documents have been leaked by school staff, we can assume the same for our school. While we can't stop the school from taking all of our data, some of it is important for them to have, but we can at least stop the school from knowing our browsing habits better than we do (and by extension, google and microsoft). This is our third goal, to protect our data, and yours too.

If you are ready to reclaim your privacy and safety, you can read our docs here, and if you aren't ready to do that just yet, we aren't going anywhere.
