FAQ

Read our about section and still have questions? well we have an FAQ to help guide you.

Who are you?

Concerned students

Are you a terrorist organization?

No, there's a clear difference between us and a terrorist organization. For one, we're not trying to instill fear, nor are we trying to harm people. Secondly, our only intent is to remove the GCPS wifi restrictions and restore BYOD access, as well as protect people from spyware that our great county uses on it's students. Our primary goal is to help people, not harm them. In fact, we've found multiple vulnurabilities on GCPS computers that should be brought to their attention, (seriously, GCPS, you really need to contact us, email renahersch@gmail.com so we can help fix some exploits).

Can I join you?

The answer is almost always no, unless you have decent technical skills, but even then you'll still have to find us, and it's going to be difficult to be trusted. We cannot risk revealing our identities to the county, as the administrators often stretch the rules as to what they can do, and could come after us. However, you can still help by messaging your local school, or emailing the school board. If enough of us complain, they surely must listen.

Which school district are you a part of?

Nice try school admins, better luck next time.

In all seriousness though, we're part of many different schools, some with wifi, some without.

Isn't what you're doing wrong and unethical?

We believe that it is exactly the opposite. The removal of BYOD especially in an age where school shootings and emergencies are common, and the school APs cannot stop bullying (or care to stop it), it is clear that being able to contact parents and loved ones in time of crisis is important. Not to mention that making everyone use crappy school chromebooks only makes things worse for everyone, as the school could spend on higher-quality chromebooks for the kids that need them, while letting others bring their own devices and save costs. We are worried that the removal of the BYOD network harms school safety and the learning environment while doing little to prevent kids from being on their phones or playing games in class.
